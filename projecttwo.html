715th Bombardment Squadron, 448th Bombardment Group: WWII

WWII History & Missions

Timeline of History & Missions during WWII:

  • 1943 - The squadron was first activated on 1 May 1943 at Gowen Field, Idaho as one of the original four squadrons of the 448th Bombardment Group. After completing initial traini
  • 1943 - The squadron flew its first combat mission from RAF Seething on 22 December 1943. it was primarily engaged in the strategic bombing campaign
  • 1944 - The squadron participated in Big Week, an intensive campaign against German aircraft manufacturing plants from 20 to 25 February 1944.
  • 1944 - It bombed V-weapon launch sites, airfields and transportation facilities to support Operation Overlord, the invasion of Normandy, and on D-Day attacked coastal defenses and choke points on German lines of communication.
  • 1944 - It struck enemy positions to assist the allied attacks on Caen and Operation Cobra, the breakout at Saint Lo.
  • 1944 - It dropped supplies to allied troops during Operation Market Garden, the attempt to seize a bridgehead across the Rhine in the Netherlands.
  • 1944-45 - During the Battle of the Bulge, it attacked transportation and communications targets in December 1944 and January 1945.
  • 1945 - In the spring of 1945, it again dropped supplies to airborne troops in Operation Varsity, the airborne assault across the Rhine near Wesel.
  • 1945 - The squadron flew its last combat mission on 25 April 1945, an attack on a railroad yard near Salzburg, Austria.
  • 1945 - The air echelon began returning to the United States with their planes in June 1945.
  • 1945 - The ground echelon sailed from Greenock on the RMS Queen Mary on 6 July.
  • 1945 - Squadron members were given leave upon arrival in the States and the squadron began to assemble at Sioux Falls Army Air Field, South Dakota in the middle of the month.
  • 1945 - After training with the Boeing B-29 Superfortress, the squadron moved to Fort Worth Army Air Field, Texas in December 1945.
  • 1946 - At Fort Worth, it became one of the first units of Strategic Air Command in March 1946.
